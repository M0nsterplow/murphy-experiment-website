The Origin

Historical Note: In the year 2020 CE, during what historians now call "The Great Pause," a respiratory pathogen designated COVID-19 forced the global population into isolation. Our research indicates that while this period devastated live performance industries, it paradoxically accelerated collaborative technology adoption.


The Murphy Experiment represents a fascinating case study from this era. Previously disconnected musicians spanning 6,797 kilometers between Tempe, Arizona and Bangor, Northern Ireland utilized primitive but effective digital audio transmission protocols to synthesize musical compositions in real-time across continental boundaries. What your ancestors called "lockdown" became their laboratory. The isolation that defined that period freed them to experiment without traditional industry constraints - a creative silver lining to an otherwise dark chapter in human history.

About

The Murphy Experiment is a sonic laboratory where musical boundaries go to die. Born from the creative chaos of a global pandemic in 2020, they blend progressive rock with humor, experimentation, and unapologetic originality into something uniquely unhinged.


Their sound careens from introspective melodies to full-throttle musical mayhem, always with a wink and a nod to the absurdity of it all.


When things get really out of hand, their sippy cup-beer drinking alter egos, The Plow Monsters, take the wheel. Fronted by the legendary Cowsick Steve and his bovine-defending anthems, The Plow Monsters prove that sometimes the best music comes from letting go of the steering wheel entirely.


The Murphy Experiment makes music for misfits, thinkers, and anyone who's ever thought "yeah, but what if we tried THIS instead?"
